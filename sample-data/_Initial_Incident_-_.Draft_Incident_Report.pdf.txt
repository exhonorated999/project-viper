Arrow Bl just west of Citrus Av.
Details:
Upon arrival, I observed Corporal Porch had conducted a traffic stop on a tractor-trailer. 
Corporal Porch was actively conducting a cargo theft investigation and requested that I 
place the driver and solo occupant of the tractor in the rear of my patrol vehicle. At this time, 
driver Arman Gevorgyan was being detained and not placed in handcuffs. 
Corporal Porch needed to conduct further investigation and requested that Arman be 
released on the scene as the tractor and trailer were being towed. Upon explaining why his 
tractor and trailer were being towed, Arman stated he had multiple cell phones and was 
missing one. 
I checked the back of my patrol vehicle, believing that Arman might have left a cell phone 
that the officers could have inadvertently missed. During this inspection, I observed 
significant damage to the rear of my patrol unit. Arman had broken a seatbelt, scratched the 
plexiglass, and bent and damaged a metal screen leading to the front seat. After noticing the 
damage, I informed Corporal Porch, who was present as Arman walked away eastbound on 
Arrow Blvd. Corporal Porch quickly assessed the damage and agreed that it was significant 
damage had been caused to a Fontana Police Department patrol vehicle.
