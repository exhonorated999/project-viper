I then inspected the attached trailer, which was bearing California license plate 4WL6788. A records check 
revealed the trailer was registered to JC Refrigerated Transport Service Incorporated out of Covina, 
California. I observed the trailer doors were not secured with a load seal, which is inconsistent with standard 
industry practice for legitimate cargo transport. I inspected the interior of the trailer and observed numerous 
pallets containing large bags filled with substantial chunks of aluminum metal. I photographed the shipping 
labels and product and contacted Senior Crime Intelligence Analyst Gary Thomas with CargoNet. After 
reviewing the photographs and shipping information, Thomas advised that the load had been reported 
stolen out of the St. Bernard Police Department in Arabi, Louisiana, under case number 2602-3325.
At the time of my discovery of the tractor and trailer, I was contacted by Eric Mize with H&S Transport and 
Recovery Inc. Mize stated he had repossession authorization from Asset Compliant Solutions (ACS) due to 
delinquent payments on the tractor. Mize subsequently emailed me a repossession authorization and hold 
harmless agreement signed by Brian Noble, President and CEO of ACS. Mize was able to gain lawful 
access to the cab of the tractor and allowed me to enter the vehicle to secure any loose personal property 
for safekeeping prior to repossession.
Shortly after recovering the load, I was contacted by Anthony Hernandez, who identified himself as the 
Director of Claims and Insurance for Steam Logistics. Anthony confirmed the freight located inside the trailer 
belonged to Steam Logistics and had in fact been stolen. Anthony estimated the value of the cargo to be 
approximately $100,000. Anthony later provide the BOL, photos of the original tractor involved as well as a 
Theft Claim Summary Report.
After confirming the cargo had been reported stolen, I contacted Armada Towing to respond to the location 
and tow the trailer pursuant to VC 22655.5 for evidentiary purposes.
